### 2. Paper 1: Flow Matching for Generative Modeling (2023)

#### Core Concepts and Contributions

Flow Matching (FM) is a methodology that fundamentally redesigns the training approach for Continuous Normalizing Flows (CNFs). It eliminates the costly ODE simulation that was a bottleneck of existing CNFs and introduces Vector Field Regression.

- **Simulation-free training:** Enables efficient large-scale training without complex ODE computation.

- **Conditional Flow Matching (CFM):** Learns conditional vector fields instead of marginal probability paths to derive optimal solutions.

- **Optimal Transport (OT) paths:** Improves both sampling speed and quality through straight-line trajectories.


#### Mathematical Definition and Comparison

The key differentiators of FM compared to existing methodologies are as follows.

- **Loss function:**

    $$L_{FM}(\theta) = E_{t, p_t(x)} ||v_t(x) - u_t(x)||^2$$

    ($v_t(x)$ is the learned network, $u_t(x)$ is the target vector field)


|**Category**|**Training Method**|**Trajectory Shape**|**Sampling Efficiency**|
|---|---|---|---|
|**Existing CNF**|Requires ODE simulation|Arbitrary curves|Low|
|**Diffusion**|SDE-based|Primarily curves|Medium|
|**Flow Matching**|Simulation-free|Straight line (with OT)|High|

#### Experimental Results and Analysis

- **Performance:** On ImageNet 64x64, the FM-OT model achieved FID 14.45, outperforming DDPM (17.36) and Score Matching (19.74).

- **Efficiency:** Thanks to the straight-line trajectory characteristics, it generates samples of equivalent quality with only about 60% of the NFE (Number of Function Evaluations) compared to Diffusion models.


#### Limitations and Future Work

- Even when conditional flows follow OT, there is no guarantee that the Marginal Vector Field from the overall distribution perspective is a global OT solution.

- Future work requires extension to conditional generation tasks such as anisotropic Gaussian path design and Super-resolution.

### 3. Paper 2: OmniV2V - Versatile Video Generation and Editing (2025)

#### Core Architecture

OmniV2V is a framework that unifies video generation and editing, using **HunyuanVideo (MM-DiT)** with Flow Matching technology as its backbone.

- **Unified Dynamic Content Manipulation:** Combines masked video and source video through a 3D-VAE-based Latent-Fusion Video Tokenizer.

- **Visual-Text Instruction Module:** Uses LLaVA to tokenize instructions, text prompts, and reference images. Notably, the approach of treating the reference image as the **-1st frame** of the video is impressive.


#### Experimental Performance (OmniV2V-Test Benchmark)

|**Metric**|**OmniV2V**|**VACE 1.3B**|**Kling 1.6**|
|---|---|---|---|
|**FVD** (Lower is better)|**900.35**|942.87|1012.54|
|**Temporal Consistency**|**0.967**|0.961|0.955|
|**Face-sim**|**0.614**|0.583|0.592|

#### Innovations and Implications

- **Unified framework:** Consolidates tasks previously handled by separate models — object replacement, Inpainting, Pose-guided generation — into a single model.

- **Lightweight condition injection:** Achieves greater efficiency by using FC layers and Token Fusion instead of complex ControlNet architectures.

- **Limitations:** Shortcomings are still observed in small object identification and complex hand gesture generation.

### 4. Comprehensive Analysis and Research Implications

**Connecting Theory to Application:**

The mathematical efficiency provided by Flow Matching (reduced training time and straight-trajectory sampling) serves as the core engine enabling large-scale video diffusion models like OmniV2V to function as practical editing tools.

**Technical Implications:**

1. **Simplification of model architecture:** Simplifying complex probabilistic processes into straight-line paths, as in Flow Matching's CFM approach, is key to training large-scale models.

2. **Multimodal control:** OmniV2V's visual-text alignment approach holds significant benchmarking value for future video upscaling and real-time video synthesis.
