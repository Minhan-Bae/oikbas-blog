**Research Topic:** Ming-Omni — Analysis of a Lightweight Multimodal SOTA Model Unifying Perception and Generation

### 1. Executive Summary

Ming-Omni (2025) is the first open-source model that simultaneously performs **perception** and **generation** across text, image, audio, and video within a single unified model. Notably, with only **2.8B active parameters**, it achieves perception performance on par with 7B-class models, and records an FID of 4.85 in image generation, surpassing SDXL. The MoE design that captures both "cost-effectiveness" and "versatility" stands out.

---

### 2. Key Technical Innovations

#### Ling: Modality-Specific Router MoE

Departing from conventional MoE that processes all tokens through a single shared router, modality-specific dedicated routers are introduced.

- **T-Router (text), A-Router (audio), V-Router (vision):** Precisely assign tokens to experts that understand each modality's unique characteristics.

- **Efficiency:** Maximizes computational efficiency while activating only 2.8B parameters, achieving knowledge density comparable to much larger models.

#### Directional Generation Framework

1. **Image Generation:** Leverages multi-scale learnable queries (4x4, 8x8, 16x16). Uses Diffusion Transformer (DiT) blocks as a bridge to achieve SOTA-level quality in text-to-image generation and editing.

2. **Audio Generation:** Compresses tokens by 36% (50Hz -> 32Hz) through a BPE-based audio decoder. Enables natural speech synthesis that considers conversational context.

### 3. Performance Evaluation and Comparison

#### Perception and Generation Benchmark Results

| **Domain** | **Benchmark** | **Ming-Omni** | **Baseline Comparison** | **Notes** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Image Understanding** | MMMU | 56.3 | Qwen2.5-VL-7B (equivalent) | Achieved with 2.8B active parameters |
| **Image Generation** | FID (lower is better) | **4.85** | SDXL (8.12) | Top open-source level |
| **Image Generation** | GenEval | 0.64 | Janus-Pro (0.67) | High instruction-following capability |
| **Video Understanding** | Average score | 59.4 | MVBench, VideoMME, etc. | Supports audiovisual integrated understanding |

#### Ming-Omni vs. Qwen3-Omni Comparative Analysis

Both models aim for "Omni" capability, but they clearly target different objectives.

| **Feature** | **Ming-Omni** | **Qwen3-Omni** |
|---|---|---|
| **Strength** | Integrated image/speech generation, lightweight | Ultra-low-latency conversation, large-scale reasoning |
| **Active Parameters** | 2.8B (efficiency-focused) | 30B (performance-focused) |
| **Core Architecture** | Modality-Specific MoE | Thinker-Talker MoE |
| **Generation Capability** | Image (DiT) and speech generation supported | Speech generation specialized (no image generation) |

### 4. Training Strategy (Two-Phase Strategy)

Ming-Omni completely separates training phases to prevent conflicts between perception and generation.

1. **Phase 1 (Perception):** Maximizes foundational perception and reasoning capabilities through a Stepwise Balancing strategy that dynamically adjusts the convergence speed of each modality.

2. **Phase 2 (Generation):** With the already-trained perception LLM frozen, only generation-specific connectors and the DiT/audio decoder are additionally trained. This is a clever approach that preserves the model's reasoning knowledge while grafting generation capabilities.

---

### 5. Implications from a Vision AI Perspective

#### Technical Insights

- **Granularity of routing:** As multimodal inputs become more complex, modality-specific routers improve expert utilization efficiency over single routers. This is empirically validated.

- **Modularization of generation modules:** When combining a perception engine (LLM) with a generation engine (DiT), using multi-scale queries as a bridge is highly effective for high-resolution image generation.

#### Practical Applications and Synergies

- **Automated content production:** Optimally suited for workflows that output narrated audio and edited images simultaneously from text input.

- **Qwen3-Omni combination scenario:** A conceivable architecture where Qwen3-Omni analyzes the user's complex intent with ultra-low latency (Thinker), then Ming-Omni generates relevant visual materials and audio at high quality as a response.

---

### 6. Future Challenges and Directions for Improvement

- **Token unification:** Currently, perception tokens and generation tokens are separated; research is needed to merge them into a single unified representation.

- **Multilingual expansion:** Performance currently biased toward English and Chinese needs to be extended to more languages.

- **Latency optimization:** Acquiring performance data for real-time streaming scenarios and introducing streaming generation techniques are required.
